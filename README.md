# Civilian Authority for Artificial Intelligence (CAIA)

The Civilian Authority for Artificial Intelligence (CAIA) is a proposed civilian-led governance framework designed to improve transparency, accountability, and safety in the development of advanced artificial intelligence systems.

Artificial intelligence is rapidly becoming foundational infrastructure affecting economies, public knowledge systems, and global security. Despite this growing influence, governance mechanisms for advanced AI remain fragmented between corporations and governments.

CAIA proposes a multi-stakeholder governance model that introduces four core institutional mechanisms:

• Global AI Model Registry  
• Certification and Safety Review Framework  
• AI Incident Reporting and Investigation System  
• Civilian-led Governance Architecture  

The goal of this framework is to encourage responsible innovation while ensuring transparency, public oversight, and international coordination in the governance of advanced AI systems.

## Purpose

This project explores a governance model that allows civilian representation alongside technical experts, institutions, and industry participants in shaping how advanced AI systems are deployed and monitored.

The document is intended as an early policy proposal designed to contribute to discussion among researchers, policymakers, technologists, and civil society.
